NextGen Move
Your next step, engineered.

A relocation-and-placement brand built on precision, motion, and quiet confidence — expressed through a boarding-pass visual language, an editorial serif, and one signature gradient used with intent.

03Gradient system
Four gradients, four jobs. "Horizon" is the signature — reserve it for the single most important moment on a page (hero, cover, primary CTA). Never run two gradients on one screen.
HORIZON — signature
Horizon
Hero banners, cover pages, the one big moment per screen. 115°, indigo → amber.
DUSK — subtle
Dusk
Highlighted card backgrounds, empty states, quiet emphasis without shouting.
ROUTE — accent line
Route
Thin accent underlines, progress bars, the journey-arc motif. 2–4px max.
INK FADE — overlay
Ink Fade
Scrims over photography/illustration so white text stays legible.

07Do & don't
DO
Use Horizon gradient once per screen, at the top, as a full-bleed banner.
DON'T
Apply the gradient to buttons, tags, and the hero all on the same screen — it stops feeling signature.
DO
Keep card padding and gaps tight (8–14px) — density reads as precision, not clutter.
DON'T
Add 24px+ gaps "for breathing room" — it dilutes the engineered, itinerary feel.
DO
Use initials avatars in the four core hues, rotated by name hash.
DON'T
Use stock photography or generic illustrated people — breaks the precision pillar.
